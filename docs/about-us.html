About us!

Who is the biological society?

The UWI Biological Society is a not for profit, environmental organization which is based at the University of the West Indies St.Augustine Campus and was offically established in April, 1987. We believe that with dedication, cooperation and sound planning, a small group can make important changes within society. We have dedicated ourselves to the protection and restoration of the environment and we will work together to encourage a similar sense of responsibility in others.

Our mission?

To promote responsible cohabitation between humans and the natural environment via the empowerment of individuals within the wider society.

Education. Restoration. Preservation.

Education, Restoration, and Preservation. The Society's activities are aimed at the education of our members as well as the wider public to the beauty & importance of nature as well as the existing threats to our natural environment, restoration of damaged or declining wildlife or habitats and the preservation of the environment. These include: nature tours, field trips, exhibition booths, lectures, workshops, turtle patrols, clean-ups, and conservation and preservation projects.

How can i take part?

Who can join?

The society consists mainly of university students and staff of any faculty, but it is open to any person interested in nature and the protection of the natural environment.

Where can we find you?

Our office is located on the ground floor of the Natural Sciences Building in the Faculty of Science and Technology(Nat Sci FST)! When entering from the Northern side of the building, we're the second door on the left(A black door with lots of vines, you can't miss it)

What do we get up to?

In addition to our General Meetings and Conservation Conversations, we also host and/or take part in Beach Clean-Ups, Hikes, Nature Walks, Educational Adventures, Sustainability Drives, Initiatives and Legislation both locally and internationally and so much more!
